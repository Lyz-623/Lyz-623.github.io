Selected Research

Line-scan Raman microscopy and denoising algorithms

Master's thesis, supervised by Prof. Perry Ping Shum and Prof. Jinna Chen

Built and studied a line-scan Raman microscopic imaging system for high-throughput, label-free chemical imaging. The platform combines line-shaped parallel excitation, scanning control, and an area-array CCD detector, allowing spatial information and Raman spectra along one line to be recorded simultaneously.

Developed a CCD-noise-model-based deep learning denoising method using simulated noisy/clean spectral pairs, and designed an end-to-end framework combining denoising and spectral unmixing for low-SNR mixed spectra.

3D drift correction for super-resolution imaging

Super-resolution fluorescence microscopy / SMLM

Worked on a 3D drift correction strategy for SMLM using a single near-infrared laser. The method uses reflected light for axial focus stabilization and speckle images for lateral drift estimation, improving robustness when localization events are sparse.
